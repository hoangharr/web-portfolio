3. Hypothetical Situations

Mastering the nuances of imaginary scenarios and expressing desires within professional academic contexts.

school

Conditionals Type 2

Structure

If + S + V-past, S + would/could + V-inf

Example Sentence

chat_bubble

"If I had more spare time, I would enroll in an advanced data science course."

lightbulb

Academic Pro-Tip

In formal writing, we often use "were" instead of "was" for all subjects in the 'if' clause. This creates a more professional tone.

history_edu

Wish structures

Structure

S + wish + S + V-past

Example

"I wish our university offered more hands-on workshops for students."

Used to express a desire for a current situation to be different.

Usage Comparison

Second Conditional

Focuses on a hypothetical condition and its potential result.

Wish Structure

Focuses solely on the regret or desire regarding the current state.
